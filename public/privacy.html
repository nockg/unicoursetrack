UniTrack Privacy Notice

For users in the United Kingdom, Germany, Malaysia, Australia, New Zealand and other supported locations

Last updated: 30 April 2026

Important note. This notice has been prepared for UniTrack as a student-facing academic productivity service. It should be reviewed if UniTrack’s functionality, user base, analytics, hosting arrangements, or commercial use materially changes.

1. Who we are

UniTrack is an academic tracking and study management application operated by SNTutors (“UniTrack”, “we”, “us” or “our”). The application allows users to organise academic information, including modules, assessments, grades, course notes, deadlines, tasks, saved hyperlinks, library resources, interface preferences and related study data.

SNTutors is the data controller for the personal information described in this notice. This means that SNTutors decides why and how that information is processed for the purposes of operating UniTrack.

Privacy, support and data rights requests should be sent to: ssnnckg@gmail.com.

Application URL: https://unicoursetrack.vercel.app/.

2. Scope of this notice

This privacy notice applies to personal information processed through UniTrack, including information used for account access, authentication, academic tracking, cloud synchronisation, backup import and export, recovery functionality, user preferences, support requests and service security.

UniTrack is designed as a private academic productivity tool. It is not currently intended to function as, or provide the features of, a public social network, marketplace, payment service, advertising platform, public media-sharing service, direct messaging platform or public profile directory.

3. Personal information we collect

Category Examples Purpose
Account and authentication data Email address, user ID, session information, login status and authentication records. To create and maintain accounts, verify access and keep users signed in securely.
Academic profile data Name, institution, course title, academic year, grading system and academic settings. To personalise the application and configure academic tracking features.
Academic tracker data Modules, assessments, predicted marks, grades, notes, deadlines, tasks, saved hyperlinks and library materials entered by the user. To store, display, organise and synchronise the user’s academic information.
Backup and recovery data Exported backup files, imported files, validation data and locally stored recovery snapshots. To allow users to restore, transfer, protect or recover their tracker data.
Preferences and settings Theme, display density, font preferences, calendar settings, dashboard layout and similar interface choices. To preserve the user’s preferred application experience.
Technical and diagnostic data IP address, device and browser information, timestamps, error logs, hosting logs and security events. To maintain availability, diagnose issues, prevent misuse and protect the application.

4. Information we do not intentionally collect

UniTrack does not intentionally request payment card details, government identification numbers, passport details, full residential addresses, precise location data, telephone numbers, biometric identifiers, health information, contact lists, profile photographs, audio recordings, video files or public social media content.

Some parts of UniTrack allow free-text input, including notes, tasks and library entries. Users should avoid entering confidential, sensitive or unnecessary personal information into those fields. If a user chooses to enter such information, it may form part of that user’s stored tracker data.

5. How we use personal information

We process personal information only for purposes connected with providing, maintaining and protecting UniTrack. These purposes include:

  • creating and managing user accounts;
  • saving, displaying, synchronising and restoring academic tracker data;
  • maintaining user preferences and application settings;
  • providing backup, import, export and recovery features;
  • responding to support, privacy and account requests;
  • preventing unauthorised access, misuse, data loss and service disruption; and
  • complying with legal, regulatory or security obligations where applicable.

We do not sell personal information. We do not use academic tracker data for advertising, behavioural profiling, targeted marketing, sale to third parties or unrelated commercial exploitation.

6. Lawful basis for processing

Where UK GDPR, EU GDPR or similar data protection laws apply, we rely on the lawful bases set out below.

Lawful basis How it applies to UniTrack
Performance of a contract Used where processing is necessary to provide the core UniTrack service requested by the user, including account access, data storage, synchronisation, import, export and application functionality.
Legitimate interests Used where processing is necessary for service reliability, security, troubleshooting, abuse prevention, product maintenance and responding to user support requests, provided those interests are not overridden by user rights.
Consent Used where consent is legally required, including for optional analytics or non-essential tracking technologies if such features are introduced.
Legal obligation Used where processing is necessary to comply with applicable law, lawful requests, regulatory requirements or legal proceedings.

7. Browser storage, cookies and similar technologies

UniTrack may use browser storage, local storage, session storage and similar technologies to keep users signed in, preserve application state, remember preferences, support backup and recovery features, and ensure the application functions correctly.

These technologies are used for service operation rather than advertising. UniTrack does not use targeted advertising cookies, cross-site advertising trackers or behavioural marketing profiles.

If optional analytics or additional cookie-based features are introduced, UniTrack will provide appropriate information and, where required by law, a valid consent mechanism before such technologies are used.

8. Service providers and recipients

We use third-party infrastructure providers to operate UniTrack. Those providers process information only as necessary to provide their services to UniTrack.

Recipient or provider Purpose
Supabase Authentication, account management, database hosting, security controls and cloud synchronisation.
Vercel Application hosting, deployment, content delivery, performance monitoring and optional usage analytics if enabled.
Legal, regulatory or security recipients Disclosure only where necessary for legal compliance, investigation of misuse, protection of rights, security incidents or valid legal proceedings.

We do not provide personal information to advertising networks, data brokers or unrelated marketing partners.

9. International data transfers

UniTrack may be accessed by users in the United Kingdom, Germany, Malaysia, Australia, New Zealand and other jurisdictions. The cloud and hosting providers used to operate UniTrack may process or store personal information outside the country in which a user is located.

Where UK GDPR or EU GDPR applies to an international transfer of personal information, we aim to rely on appropriate safeguards or recognised transfer mechanisms. These may include adequacy regulations or decisions, standard contractual clauses, the UK International Data Transfer Agreement or UK Addendum, processor terms, data processing agreements, and provider security commitments, depending on the relevant transfer and provider arrangements.

10. Retention of personal information

Data type Retention approach
Account and cloud tracker data Retained while the account remains active, or until the user deletes the data or requests deletion, subject to limited retention where necessary for legal, security or operational reasons.
Browser and local storage Stored on the user’s device until cleared by the user, removed by browser settings, replaced by later application data or removed when signing out or resetting local data.
Exported backups Controlled by the user after download. UniTrack does not control backup copies stored outside the application by the user.
Local recovery snapshots Stored locally for recovery purposes and normally replaced by newer snapshots or removed when local browser data is cleared.
Technical and security logs Retained only for as long as reasonably necessary for reliability, security, troubleshooting, provider operations or legal compliance.

11. Security

We use technical and organisational measures intended to protect personal information in a manner proportionate to the nature and scale of UniTrack. These measures may include account authentication, access controls, cloud database segregation, Supabase row-level security where configured, validation of server-side requests, restrictions on payload size, safeguards against accidental credential exposure, and monitoring of technical errors or misuse.

No online service can guarantee absolute security. Users should use a secure email account, protect login credentials, avoid sharing accounts, and take care when entering confidential information into free-text fields.

12. User controls within UniTrack

Control Function
Export backup Allows a user to download a copy of tracker data and settings.
Import backup Allows a user to restore tracker data from a previously exported file after validation.
Download recovery backup Allows access to the most recent automatic recovery backup created before certain risky actions.
Delete cloud data Allows a user to remove cloud-stored tracker profile data. This does not delete downloaded backups or browser-local copies.
Edit profile and settings Allows a user to update academic profile details, grading settings and interface preferences.

  • be informed about how personal information is used;
  • request access to personal information;
  • request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • request deletion of personal information;
  • restrict or object to certain processing;
  • request data portability, where applicable;
  • withdraw consent where processing is based on consent; and
  • complain to a data protection regulator or supervisory authority.

Requests should be sent to ssnnckg@gmail.com. We may need to verify that the request comes from the account holder before acting on it. Some rights may be limited where an exemption applies, where data has already been deleted, where we must retain information for legal or security reasons, or where we cannot reasonably identify the relevant data.

14. Jurisdiction-specific information

14.1 United Kingdom

Where UK data protection law applies, users may have rights under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. Users may complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office if they are not satisfied with how their personal information has been handled.

14.2 Germany and the European Union / European Economic Area

Where UniTrack is used by individuals in Germany or another EU/EEA country and EU data protection law applies, references to “GDPR” include Regulation (EU) 2016/679, commonly referred to in Germany as the Datenschutz-Grundverordnung or DSGVO.

EU/EEA users may have rights under Articles 12 to 22 of the GDPR, including the right of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and the right not to be subject to certain solely automated decisions where applicable. Users may also withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.

Users in Germany may lodge a complaint with a competent German data protection supervisory authority. The competent authority may depend on the user’s federal state, the nature of the processing and the organisation involved. This right is in addition to the ability to contact us directly.

SNTutors has not appointed a data protection officer for UniTrack at this stage. Privacy requests should be directed to the contact address stated in this notice. If UniTrack’s activities change in a way that requires a data protection officer, EU representative or additional local compliance arrangements, this notice will be updated accordingly.

14.3 Malaysia

Where Malaysian data protection law applies, users may have rights to access and correct personal data and may exercise rights available under the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 and related requirements.

14.4 Australia

Where Australian privacy law applies, users may request access to and correction of personal information and may complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner where appropriate.

14.5 New Zealand

Where New Zealand privacy law applies, users may request access to and correction of personal information and may complain to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner where appropriate.

15. Children and younger users

UniTrack is primarily intended for students and young adults who can manage their own academic information. It is not designed for children under 13 years of age.

If a user is below the age at which their country allows them to use online services independently, the user should use UniTrack only with appropriate parent or guardian consent and supervision. We aim to collect only information that is reasonably necessary for the service and to explain our practices in clear language.

16. Data breach notification

If we become aware of a security incident affecting personal information, we will investigate the matter, take appropriate steps to contain and remedy the issue, and notify affected users and regulators where required by applicable law. The timing and content of any notification will depend on the nature of the incident, the risk to users and the legal requirements that apply.

We may update this notice to reflect changes to UniTrack, service providers, security practices, legal requirements or the way personal information is processed. Where a change is material, we will take reasonable steps to draw it to users’ attention, such as by in-application notice or email where feasible. The “Last updated” date at the top of this document will indicate when the notice was last revised.
